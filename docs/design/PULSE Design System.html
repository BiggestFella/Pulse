Principles.

§ 01
  1. 01

    Make it loud.

    One thing on the screen should be huge. Numbers, verbs, names. If it isn't shouting, it isn't earning its pixels.

  2. 02

    Color is mood.

    Themes aren't decoration — they set the emotional temperature. Heat for daylight, Volt for hype, Deep for focus.

  3. 03

    Numbers want to be giants.

    Tall, narrow, tabular. Stats, counts, scores — render them in Oswald Condensed and let them carry the screen.

  4. 04

    Buttons want to be pressed.

    Chunky, drop-shadowed, pill-shaped. A press travels down and back. The interface should feel like a physical object.

The rules

One thing is hero.

The giant numeral or word carries the screen. Everything else clings to it.

Labels tuck in.

Mono caps anchor to the top-right of the hero numeral. Don't float them off in their own card.

Stretch on purpose.

Use scaleY(1.2–1.4) for poster moments. Don't apply globally.

Buttons.

§ 05 · pressable, chunky, alive

Pills with a solid drop. Top edge gets a light inner highlight, bottom a dark inner shadow — so the button reads as a physical object. On press it travels down five pixels and the under-shadow collapses. Try one.
